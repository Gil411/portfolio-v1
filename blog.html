In the sea of annual film releases, faith-based stories like Ordinary Angels often go unnoticed. This film brings a refreshing look at the journey of personal transformation, highlighting resilience and hope through a moving narrative. It explores how faith not only uplifts individuals but also drives profound personal change, resonating with audiences on a universal level.

Alien: Romulus revives the franchise's signature claustrophobic horror, delivering relentless tension and suspense. The new cast brings vulnerability that amplifies the fear as they face the iconic Xenomorph. While not groundbreaking, the film balances eerie atmosphere with gripping action, making it a satisfying watch for both long-time fans and newcomers.

The Big Sick (2017) Review
  • Posted on October 29, 2024
  • by Gil
  • 55 comments
  • #Comedy
  • #Romance

The Big Sick is a heartwarming romantic comedy based on comedian Kumail Nanjiani's real-life love story. Blending humor with touching moments, it explores cultural clashes, family expectations, and love's complexities. Kumail's journey is both funny and moving, with standout performances from Holly Hunter and Ray Romano as Emily's parents adding warmth and authenticity. Genuine and relatable, The Big Sick shines as a unique take on modern romance.

Like Stars on Earth (2007) Review
  • Posted on October 29, 2024
  • by Gil
  • 12 comments
  • #Musical
  • #Kids
  • #Family

Like Stars on Earth (2007) is a heartfelt story about Ishaan, a young boy with dyslexia, whose life changes when a compassionate teacher helps him embrace his unique gifts. Directed by Aamir Khan, the film shines a light on the importance of empathy in education, capturing the beauty of individuality and the power of understanding. This film is a moving reminder of the potential within every child.

  • Posted on October 28, 2024
  • by Gil
  • 44 comments
  • #Animation
  • #Family

The Wild Robot (2024) beautifully adapts Peter Brown's story, following Roz, a robot learning to survive and connect with nature on a remote island. With stunning animation and touching themes of resilience and friendship, this family-friendly film is both an adventure and a reflection on the harmony between technology and the natural world.
